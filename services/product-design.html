Product Design FAQs

Common questions about our product design services

UX (User Experience) design focuses on the overall feel and functionality of a product, including user research, information architecture, and user flows. UI (User Interface) design focuses on the visual aspects - colors, typography, spacing, and interactive elements. Think of UX as the blueprint of a house (layout, flow) and UI as the interior design (colors, furniture). We provide both services, ensuring that beautiful interfaces (UI) are built on top of solid user experience foundations (UX).

Project timelines vary based on complexity and scope. A simple website redesign might take 4-6 weeks, while a comprehensive product design with research, multiple platforms, and design system could take 8-16 weeks. During discovery, we provide a detailed timeline based on your specific requirements. We work in sprints (typically 2 weeks) with regular reviews and feedback sessions, allowing for adjustments and ensuring we're always aligned with your vision.

We use industry-standard tools including Figma for UI design and prototyping, Miro for collaborative workshops and research synthesis, Adobe Creative Suite for asset creation, Sketch for legacy projects, InVision for prototyping and user testing, UserTesting for remote usability testing, Hotjar for analytics and heatmaps, Storybook for design system documentation, and various accessibility testing tools. We're tool-agnostic and can work with whatever your team prefers or migrate to better solutions when needed.

Yes, we provide comprehensive design system implementation support including: detailed design specifications, component documentation in tools like Storybook, developer handoff with precise measurements and specs, accessibility guidelines and testing, implementation reviews and quality assurance, training sessions for your development team, ongoing maintenance and versioning support, and integration with your development workflow. We ensure that the design system is not just delivered but successfully adopted and maintained by your team.
